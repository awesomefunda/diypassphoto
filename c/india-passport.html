Official India photo requirements ↗

India Passport photo requirements

51×51 mm (2×2 inches), plain white background, face covering most of the frame. Verified from Passport Seva / Ministry of External Affairs (passportindia.gov.in).

  • 51×51 mm (2×2 inches), colour JPEG, under 500 KB
  • Face should cover 70–80% of the photo area, centred
  • Plain white background, no shadows or patterns
  • Neutral expression, both eyes open
  • No glasses recommended
  • Religious head coverings allowed if face is clearly visible
  • Taken within the last 6 months

Common rejection reasons

  • Off-white, cream, or shadowed background
  • Face too small or off-centre
  • Glasses causing glare or shadow
  • Shadows on the background
  • Photo older than 6 months

Frequently asked questions

What size is an Indian passport photo?

51×51 mm (2×2 inches) with a plain white background. Some Passport Seva services also accept 45×35 mm — always check the specific form.

Are glasses allowed in an Indian passport photo?

Glasses are not recommended but not explicitly banned. If worn, they must not create glare or shadow on the eyes.

Are head coverings allowed?

Religious head coverings are permitted as long as the full face — from forehead to chin, ear to ear — is clearly visible.
