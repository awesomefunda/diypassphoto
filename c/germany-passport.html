Official Germany photo requirements ↗

Germany Passport photo requirements

35 mm wide × 45 mm tall, light grey background, face 32–36 mm, no glasses. Germany uses the ISO/IEC 19794-5 biometric standard. Verified from Bundesdruckerei (bundesdruckerei.de).

  • 35 mm wide × 45 mm tall, colour, JPEG
  • Face 32–36 mm from chin to crown (70–80% of height)
  • Light grey background (not pure white — ISO biometric standard)
  • Neutral expression, mouth closed, eyes open and looking forward
  • No glasses (banned since January 2017 for German documents)
  • No head coverings (except for documented religious reasons)
  • Even lighting, no shadows on face or background
  • Taken within the last 6 months

Common rejection reasons

  • Pure white background — German biometric standard requires light grey
  • Glasses in the photo (not allowed since 2017)
  • Face outside 32–36 mm
  • Shadows or uneven lighting
  • Smiling or open mouth

Frequently asked questions

What background does a German passport photo need?

A light grey background — not pure white. Germany follows the ISO biometric standard which specifies a uniformly light grey background to ensure consistent face detection.

Are glasses allowed in a German passport photo?

No. Germany banned glasses in passport and ID photos since January 2017, with no exceptions for prescription glasses.

What size is a German passport photo?

35 mm wide × 45 mm tall (the standard European biometric format), with the face 32–36 mm from chin to crown.
