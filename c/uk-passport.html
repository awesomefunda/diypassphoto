Official United Kingdom photo requirements ↗

UK Passport photo requirements

35×45 mm, plain light-grey or cream background, head 29–34 mm. Digital: at least 600×750 px, 50 KB–10 MB. Verified from HM Passport Office (gov.uk).

  • 35 mm wide × 45 mm tall; digital at least 600×750 px, 50 KB to 10 MB
  • Head 29–34 mm from chin to crown (64–76% of the 45 mm height)
  • Plain light-grey or cream background — not pure white
  • Plain expression, mouth closed, eyes open and visible
  • No glasses unless medically required (tinted lenses not permitted)
  • No head coverings unless worn for religious or medical reasons
  • Clear, in-focus, colour photo — not altered by software

Common rejection reasons

  • Pure white background (light grey or cream is required by HMPO)
  • Head outside the 29–34 mm range
  • Hair across the eyes
  • Shadows or reflections on the face or background
  • Photo edited with software or filters

Frequently asked questions

What size is a UK passport photo?

45 mm tall × 35 mm wide for prints. Digital photos must be at least 600 pixels wide and 750 pixels tall, between 50 KB and 10 MB.

Does a UK passport photo need a white background?

No — HM Passport Office requires a plain light-grey or cream background, not pure white. The background must contrast clearly with your face.

How big should my head be in a UK passport photo?

Your head (chin to crown) must be 29–34 mm in a 45 mm photo — roughly 64–76% of the photo height.

Are glasses allowed in a UK passport photo?

Generally no. Glasses are only permitted if removal is medically impossible, and must not have tinted lenses or cause glare.
