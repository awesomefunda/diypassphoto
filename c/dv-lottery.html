US DV Lottery photo requirements

Square 600–1200 px, JPEG, under 240 KB, white background, head 50–69%. The DV entry form won't warn you if your background or head size is wrong — it accepts the upload and rejects you later. DIYPassPhoto catches it first. Verified from the US State Department.

  • Square (1:1), 600×600 to 1200×1200 px, JPEG format
  • File size 240 KB or less
  • Head 50–69% of image height (chin to crown)
  • Plain white or off-white background, no shadows
  • Neutral expression, eyes open, one person only
  • No glasses, no head coverings (unless religious)
  • Taken within the last 6 months, no AI edits

Common rejection reasons

  • Accepted at upload then rejected later — form does not check background or head size
  • Background not truly white (grey wall, shadow, pattern)
  • File over 240 KB or not exactly square
  • Head outside the 50–69% band
  • AI-edited photo — not allowed for US documents since 2026

Frequently asked questions

What are the DV Lottery photo requirements?

Square (1:1), 600×600 to 1200×1200 pixels, JPEG, under 240 KB, plain white background, head 50–69% of height, taken within 6 months.

Why did my photo pass the upload but get rejected?

The DV entry form only checks file size and format. It does not detect a bad background or wrong head size — those are caught later at human review.

Is the DV Lottery photo the same as a passport photo?

Same dimensions and rules, but the DV Lottery adds a strict 240 KB file-size cap for digital submission. DIYPassPhoto formats and compresses to spec automatically.
